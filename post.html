Mar y Montaña

Posted on February 25, 2025 by Roman

It's been a while, hasn't it?

I'm distant from everyone again. I'm popping modafinil again.

I know, I know. I know. Not good.

Old habits have a way of greeting me like old friends - little white flags of surrender swallowed with mineral water between swirls of turbulence.

My throat is perpetually dry from flights into strange time zones. The past couple of weeks have been a blur. I miss home. I miss many things. I'm writing to you from a boardroom where I'll be seeing Uli for the first time since Art Basel, he's the one to thank for the rides to and from hotels these past few weeks. If I hadn't run into his daughter in St. Moritz I'd probably have spent a small fortune on Ubers and Addison Lee's by now. Turns out we have mutuals, I guess. I blame the serendipity of the apres-ski crowd.

Sam arrived today. We talked about DACA over coffee and he tells me about his newborn. He's one of the good ones. Though I wonder - what does it feel like to be a father in this kind of world?

I feel like I'm losing my soul these days. A friend told me she kneeled in a cathedral, hair covered in a silk veil, to pray for me the other day.
Another friend said they'd read my heart in a tarot deck, glyphs spread across black marble and noble silver somewhere in the Cotswolds.
Yet another friend texted me to tell me that they'd recited a protection spell under moonlight - under burning mountain sage and crushed sea salt. I was given instructions each time, all eerily similar. Bow my head and say the name seven times, light the myhrr and say a prayer.

I tend to have more questions than answers these days, a new world is emerging. I'll write that one soon, I suppose.
I guess you probably have more questions than answers too. Maybe someday I'll answer them all but for now all I'd like to know is how long before a soul can't be kept whole anymore? How far is too far in a holy war?
